# Drawbacks of ISO 17799 / BS 7799

## Introduction

**ISO 17799** (now ISO/IEC 27002) and **BS 7799** are important standards for Information Security Management. They provide guidelines and best practices for securing information assets. However, these standards also have some limitations and drawbacks.

## Drawbacks of ISO 17799 / BS 7799

### 1. High Implementation Cost

* Implementing the standards requires significant investment.
* Organizations may need new security tools, training, and consultants.

### 2. Time-Consuming Process

* Developing policies, conducting risk assessments, and implementing controls take considerable time.
* Certification and audits also require additional effort.

### 3. Complex Documentation

* A large amount of documentation is required.
* Maintaining records and security documents can be difficult.

### 4. Not Technology-Specific

* The standards provide general guidelines rather than detailed technical solutions.
* Organizations must decide how to implement controls themselves.

### 5. Difficult for Small Organizations

* Small businesses may lack the resources, budget, and expertise needed for implementation.
* Compliance can become a burden for smaller organizations.

### 6. Requires Continuous Maintenance

* Security policies and controls must be regularly reviewed and updated.
* Continuous monitoring and auditing are necessary.

### 7. No Guarantee of Complete Security

* Following the standards does not eliminate all security risks.
* New threats and attacks may still occur.

### 8. Employee Resistance

* Employees may find security rules restrictive.
* Lack of awareness or cooperation can reduce effectiveness.

### 9. Certification Costs

* External audits and certification processes can be expensive.
* Periodic recertification adds further costs.

## Advantages Despite Drawbacks

* Improves information security management.
* Helps identify and manage risks.
* Increases customer trust.
* Supports legal and regulatory compliance.

## Conclusion

Although **ISO 17799 and BS 7799** provide valuable security guidelines, they have drawbacks such as high cost, complexity, time-consuming implementation, and continuous maintenance requirements. However, when properly implemented, they significantly improve an organization's information security posture.
