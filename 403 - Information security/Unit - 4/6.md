# NIST Security Models

## Introduction

The **National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)** is a U.S. government organization that develops standards and guidelines for information security. NIST provides security models and frameworks that help organizations protect their information systems and manage security risks effectively.

## NIST Security Model

The NIST Security Model is based on the **CIA Triad**, which represents the three fundamental objectives of information security.

### 1. Confidentiality

* Ensures that information is accessible only to authorized users.
* Prevents unauthorized disclosure of sensitive data.
* Achieved through authentication, encryption, and access controls.

**Example:** Only authorized employees can access salary records.

### 2. Integrity

* Ensures that information remains accurate, complete, and unaltered.
* Prevents unauthorized modification of data.
* Achieved through hashing, digital signatures, and access control.

**Example:** Exam results cannot be changed by unauthorized users.

### 3. Availability

* Ensures that information and systems are available when needed.
* Protects against system failures and denial-of-service attacks.
* Achieved through backups, redundancy, and disaster recovery plans.

**Example:** A bank's online service remains available 24×7.

## NIST Security Framework Layers

### 1. Identify

* Identify organizational assets, risks, and vulnerabilities.
* Understand what needs protection.

### 2. Protect

* Implement safeguards to secure systems and data.
* Includes access control, training, and data security.

### 3. Detect

* Monitor systems to identify security incidents quickly.
* Includes intrusion detection and security monitoring.

### 4. Respond

* Take action when a security incident occurs.
* Includes incident handling and damage control.

### 5. Recover

* Restore normal operations after an incident.
* Includes backup restoration and business continuity planning.

## Benefits of NIST Security Model

* Provides a structured approach to security.
* Helps manage and reduce risks.
* Improves protection of information assets.
* Supports incident response and recovery.
* Widely accepted and used worldwide.

## Conclusion

The **NIST Security Model** focuses on protecting information through the **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA) Triad** and a risk-based security framework. It helps organizations identify, protect, detect, respond to, and recover from security threats, ensuring effective information security management.
