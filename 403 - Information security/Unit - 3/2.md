# Explain Risk Identification and Risk Assessment

## Introduction

Risk Management is an important part of Information Security. Two major steps in risk management are **Risk Identification** and **Risk Assessment**. These steps help organizations recognize potential threats and determine their impact on information assets.

---

# 1. Risk Identification

## Definition

**Risk Identification** is the process of finding and listing all possible threats, vulnerabilities, and risks that may affect an organization's information systems and assets.

The purpose is to understand what could go wrong and what needs protection.

---

## Activities in Risk Identification

### 1. Identify Assets

Determine valuable assets that need protection.

**Examples:**

* Data and databases
* Hardware
* Software
* Networks
* Employees

### 2. Identify Threats

Find events that could cause harm.

**Examples:**

* Malware
* Hacking
* Phishing
* Fire
* Flood

### 3. Identify Vulnerabilities

Determine weaknesses that can be exploited.

**Examples:**

* Weak passwords
* Outdated software
* Unsecured networks

### 4. Document Risks

Prepare a list of identified risks for further analysis.

---

## Example

* **Asset:** Customer Database
* **Threat:** Hacker Attack
* **Vulnerability:** Weak Passwords
* **Risk:** Unauthorized access to customer information

---

# 2. Risk Assessment

## Definition

**Risk Assessment** is the process of analyzing identified risks to determine their likelihood of occurrence and the impact they may have on the organization.

It helps prioritize risks and decide which risks need immediate attention.

---

## Steps in Risk Assessment

### 1. Analyze Likelihood

Estimate how likely a threat is to occur.

**Example:**

* High
* Medium
* Low

### 2. Analyze Impact

Determine the damage that may occur if the risk becomes a reality.

**Examples:**

* Financial loss
* Data loss
* Reputation damage

### 3. Determine Risk Level

Combine likelihood and impact to calculate overall risk.

| Likelihood | Impact | Risk Level  |
| ---------- | ------ | ----------- |
| High       | High   | High Risk   |
| High       | Low    | Medium Risk |
| Low        | Low    | Low Risk    |

### 4. Prioritize Risks

Focus first on high-risk issues and then address medium and low risks.

---

## Risk Assessment Methods

### a) Qualitative Risk Assessment

Uses descriptive terms such as:

* High
* Medium
* Low

### b) Quantitative Risk Assessment

Uses numerical values and financial estimates to calculate risk.

**Example:** Estimating monetary loss from a cyber attack.

---

## Difference Between Risk Identification and Risk Assessment

| Risk Identification                     | Risk Assessment                                   |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Identifies threats and vulnerabilities. | Evaluates the severity of risks.                  |
| First step in risk management.          | Follows risk identification.                      |
| Answers "What can go wrong?"            | Answers "How serious is the risk?"                |
| Creates a list of risks.                | Prioritizes risks based on impact and likelihood. |

---

## Conclusion

**Risk Identification** involves discovering assets, threats, and vulnerabilities that may affect an organization, while **Risk Assessment** evaluates the likelihood and impact of those risks. Together, these processes help organizations prioritize security efforts and protect their information assets effectively.
