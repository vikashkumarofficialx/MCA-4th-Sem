# Explain Periodic Review in Risk Management

## Introduction

Risk management is a continuous process. New threats, vulnerabilities, technologies, and business requirements may arise over time. Therefore, organizations must regularly review their risks and security controls.

**Periodic Review** ensures that risk management activities remain effective and up to date.

---

## Definition

**Periodic Review** is the process of regularly examining and evaluating risks, security controls, policies, and procedures to ensure they continue to protect organizational assets effectively.

---

## Objectives of Periodic Review

1. Identify new threats and vulnerabilities.
2. Evaluate the effectiveness of existing security controls.
3. Ensure compliance with security policies and regulations.
4. Update risk assessments according to changes in the organization.
5. Improve overall information security.

---

## Process of Periodic Review

### 1. Review Existing Risks

* Examine previously identified risks.
* Check whether risk levels have changed.

### 2. Identify New Risks

* Detect new threats and vulnerabilities.
* Consider changes in technology, business processes, and regulations.

### 3. Evaluate Security Controls

* Verify whether existing controls are working properly.
* Identify weaknesses in security measures.

### 4. Reassess Risk Levels

* Analyze likelihood and impact again.
* Recalculate risk priorities if necessary.

### 5. Update Risk Treatment Plans

* Modify security controls and strategies.
* Implement additional safeguards if required.

### 6. Document Findings

* Record review results and recommendations.
* Maintain proper audit trails and reports.

---

## Importance of Periodic Review

### 1. Adapts to Changing Threats

New cyber threats emerge continuously, requiring updated security measures.

### 2. Maintains Security Effectiveness

Ensures that implemented controls continue to provide adequate protection.

### 3. Supports Compliance

Helps organizations meet legal, regulatory, and policy requirements.

### 4. Reduces Security Risks

Early identification of weaknesses prevents major security incidents.

### 5. Improves Decision-Making

Provides updated information for management and security planning.

---

## Example

Suppose an organization installed antivirus software two years ago.

During a periodic review:

* New malware threats are identified.
* The antivirus version is found to be outdated.
* The organization updates the software and improves security controls.

This reduces the risk of malware attacks.

## Benefits of Periodic Review

1. Keeps risk assessments current.
2. Identifies emerging threats.
3. Improves security controls.
4. Ensures business continuity.
5. Reduces chances of security breaches.
6. Enhances overall organizational security.

---

## Conclusion

**Periodic Review** is a vital part of Risk Management that involves regularly evaluating risks, security controls, and policies. It helps organizations identify new threats, improve security measures, maintain compliance, and ensure continuous protection of information assets.
