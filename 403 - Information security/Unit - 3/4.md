# Process of Assessing and Controlling Risk

## Introduction

Risk Assessment and Risk Control are important parts of **Risk Management**. They help organizations identify security risks, evaluate their impact, and apply appropriate controls to protect information assets.

The goal is to reduce risks to an acceptable level and ensure the security of organizational resources.

---

## Process of Assessing and Controlling Risk

### 1. Identify Assets

The first step is to identify assets that need protection.

**Examples:**

* Data and databases
* Hardware
* Software
* Networks
* Human resources

**Purpose:** Determine what is valuable to the organization.

---

### 2. Identify Threats

Identify possible events that can cause harm to assets.

**Examples:**

* Malware
* Hacking
* Insider attacks
* Fire and flood
* Power failure

**Purpose:** Understand potential dangers.

---

### 3. Identify Vulnerabilities

Find weaknesses that can be exploited by threats.

**Examples:**

* Weak passwords
* Unpatched software
* Lack of backups
* Misconfigured systems

**Purpose:** Discover security gaps.

---

### 4. Assess Risk

Analyze each identified risk by considering:

* **Likelihood** of occurrence
* **Impact** if the risk occurs

### Risk Formula

**Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact**

**Purpose:** Determine the severity of each risk.

---

### 5. Prioritize Risks

Classify risks according to their seriousness.

| Risk Level | Description               |
| ---------- | ------------------------- |
| High       | Immediate action required |
| Medium     | Action required soon      |
| Low        | Can be monitored          |

**Purpose:** Focus on the most critical risks first.

---

### 6. Select Risk Control Strategy

Choose an appropriate method to handle the risk.

#### a) Risk Avoidance

Eliminate the activity causing the risk.

#### b) Risk Reduction (Mitigation)

Apply security controls to reduce risk.

#### c) Risk Transfer

Transfer risk to another party (e.g., insurance).

#### d) Risk Acceptance

Accept the risk if it is minor and manageable.

---

### 7. Implement Security Controls

Apply selected controls.

**Examples:**

* Firewalls
* Antivirus software
* Encryption
* Access controls
* Security policies

**Purpose:** Protect systems and information.

---

### 8. Monitor and Review

* Continuously monitor risks and controls.
* Conduct audits and security reviews.
* Update controls when new threats emerge.

**Purpose:** Maintain effective security over time.

---

---

## Benefits of Risk Assessment and Control

1. Protects organizational assets.
2. Reduces security incidents.
3. Minimizes financial losses.
4. Improves decision-making.
5. Ensures business continuity.
6. Enhances overall information security.

---

## Conclusion

The **Process of Assessing and Controlling Risk** involves identifying assets, threats, and vulnerabilities, evaluating risks, selecting appropriate control strategies, implementing security measures, and continuously monitoring the environment. This process helps organizations minimize risks and protect their information assets effectively.
