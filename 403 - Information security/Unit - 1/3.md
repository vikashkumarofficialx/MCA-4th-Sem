# What is Information Security? Explain its Objectives.

## Information Security

Information Security (InfoSec) is the process of protecting information and information systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, destruction, or disruption.

Its main purpose is to ensure that information remains safe, accurate, and available only to authorized users.

**Definition:**
Information Security is the protection of information and information assets through the use of technology, policies, procedures, and controls.

---

## Objectives of Information Security

The main objectives of Information Security are known as the **CIA Triad**:

### 1. Confidentiality

Confidentiality means protecting information from unauthorized access.

* Only authorized persons can access the data.
* Prevents leakage of sensitive information.
* Achieved through passwords, encryption, and access control.

**Example:** Bank account details should be accessible only to the account holder and authorized bank staff.

---

### 2. Integrity

Integrity means ensuring that information remains accurate, complete, and unaltered.

* Data should not be modified without permission.
* Protects against accidental or intentional changes.
* Achieved through hashing, checksums, and digital signatures.

**Example:** A student's marks should not be changed by an unauthorized person.

---

### 3. Availability

Availability means ensuring that information and resources are accessible whenever needed by authorized users.

* Systems should remain operational and accessible.
* Prevents service interruptions.
* Achieved through backups, redundancy, and disaster recovery plans.

**Example:** An online banking website should be available to customers 24×7.

---

## Other Important Objectives

### 4. Authentication

Authentication verifies the identity of a user before granting access.

**Example:** Login using username and password or biometric verification.

---

### 5. Authorization

Authorization determines what actions an authenticated user is allowed to perform.

**Example:** An employee can view records but cannot modify them.

---

### 6. Accountability

Accountability ensures that users are responsible for their actions.

* Maintains logs and audit trails.
* Helps identify who performed a particular action.

**Example:** System logs showing which user accessed a file.

---

### 7. Non-Repudiation

Non-repudiation ensures that a user cannot deny performing a particular action.

* Achieved using digital signatures and transaction records.

**Example:** A customer cannot deny sending an online transaction after digitally signing it.

---

## Conclusion

Information Security is the protection of information from threats and unauthorized access. Its primary objectives are **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA Triad)**, along with **Authentication, Authorization, Accountability, and Non-Repudiation**, which together ensure the security and reliability of information systems.
