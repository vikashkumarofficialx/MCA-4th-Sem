# Integrity Policies

## Introduction

**Integrity** is one of the three core principles of information security (CIA Triad). It ensures that information remains **accurate, complete, and unaltered** by unauthorized users. Integrity policies are security rules and models designed to prevent unauthorized modification of data and maintain its correctness.

---

## Definition

An **Integrity Policy** is a set of rules that ensures information can only be modified by authorized users and remains accurate, reliable, and consistent throughout its life cycle.

---

## Objectives of Integrity Policies

* Protect data from unauthorized modification.
* Maintain accuracy and consistency of information.
* Prevent fraud and data corruption.
* Ensure trustworthy information for decision-making.
* Control who can create, modify, or delete data.

---

## Integrity Models

### 1. Biba Integrity Model

The **Biba Model** is the most widely used integrity model. It focuses on protecting data integrity by preventing unauthorized changes.

### Main Rules of Biba Model

#### (a) Simple Integrity Rule (No Read Down)

A subject cannot read data from a lower integrity level.

**Example:**
A manager should not use untrusted information from an unknown source.

#### (b) * (Star) Integrity Rule (No Write Up)

A subject cannot write information to a higher integrity level.

**Example:**
A junior employee cannot modify important management records.

#### (c) Invocation Property

A subject cannot request services from a higher integrity level.

---


---

### 2. Clark-Wilson Integrity Model

The **Clark-Wilson Model** focuses on maintaining data integrity through well-defined procedures and separation of duties.

### Features

* Uses authorized transactions.
* Prevents unauthorized data modifications.
* Enforces separation of duties.
* Requires auditing and logging of activities.

### Example

In a bank, one employee enters a transaction while another employee approves it.

---

## Elements of Integrity Policies

### 1. Access Control

Only authorized users can modify data.

### 2. Authentication

Users must verify their identity before accessing information.

### 3. Authorization

Users receive only the permissions necessary for their work.

### 4. Auditing

All modifications are recorded for future verification.

### 5. Data Validation

Checks are performed to ensure data accuracy before storage.
