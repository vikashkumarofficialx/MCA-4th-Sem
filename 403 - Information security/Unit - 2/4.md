# Types of Threats and Attacks

## Introduction

A **threat** is any potential danger that can harm an information system or organization. An **attack** is an action taken to exploit a vulnerability and cause damage to information, systems, or networks. Understanding different types of threats and attacks is essential for effective information security.

---

## Types of Threats

### 1. Natural Threats

These threats occur due to natural disasters.

**Examples:**

* Floods
* Earthquakes
* Fire
* Storms
* Lightning

**Impact:**
Can damage computer systems, networks, and data centers.

---

### 2. Human Threats

These threats are caused by human actions, either intentional or unintentional.

#### (a) Intentional Threats

* Hacking
* Data theft
* Cyber terrorism
* Sabotage

#### (b) Unintentional Threats

* Human errors
* Accidental deletion of files
* Incorrect system configuration

---

### 3. Environmental Threats

These threats arise from environmental conditions.

**Examples:**

* Power failure
* Excessive heat
* Dust
* Water leakage

**Impact:**
Can affect hardware and system performance.

---

## Types of Attacks

### 1. Passive Attack

In a passive attack, the attacker monitors or accesses information without modifying it.

**Examples:**

* Eavesdropping
* Traffic analysis
* Monitoring network communication

**Characteristics:**

* Difficult to detect
* Information remains unchanged

---

### 2. Active Attack

In an active attack, the attacker modifies, destroys, or disrupts information.

**Examples:**

* Data modification
* Denial of Service (DoS)
* Malware attacks

**Characteristics:**

* Easier to detect than passive attacks
* Causes direct damage

---

## Common Security Attacks

### 1. Malware Attack

Malware is malicious software designed to damage systems.

**Examples:**

* Virus
* Worm
* Trojan Horse
* Ransomware
* Spyware

---

### 2. Phishing Attack

Attackers send fake emails or messages to steal passwords and sensitive information.

**Example:**
A fake bank email asking for login credentials.

---

### 3. Denial of Service (DoS) Attack

The attacker floods a server or network with excessive requests, making services unavailable to legitimate users.

---

### 4. Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attack

An attacker secretly intercepts communication between two parties.

**Impact:**
Can steal sensitive information such as passwords and banking details.

---

### 5. Password Attack

The attacker attempts to obtain user passwords.

**Methods:**

* Brute Force Attack
* Dictionary Attack
* Credential Theft

---

### 6. SQL Injection Attack

Malicious SQL commands are inserted into web application input fields to access or manipulate database data.

---

### 7. Spoofing Attack

The attacker pretends to be a trusted user or system.

**Examples:**

* IP Spoofing
* Email Spoofing
* Website Spoofing

---

## Difference Between Threat and Attack

| Threat                                | Attack                                 |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Potential danger to a system          | Actual attempt to cause harm           |
| May or may not occur                  | Actively executed                      |
| Creates risk                          | Exploits vulnerability                 |
| Example: Hacker planning an intrusion | Example: Hacker breaking into a system |

---

## Conclusion

Threats and attacks pose serious risks to information systems. Threats can be natural, human, or environmental, while attacks may be passive or active. Organizations must implement proper security controls to protect their systems, data, and networks from these threats and attacks.
